#Holiday #Humanity

I'm not doing the New Year's resolution thing this year. I'd say I'm resolving not to do it but that would be, you know, counterproductive. So instead this year I'm making a New Year's wish. I wish to be absurdly rich, monetarily speaking.

A little self-centered? Perhaps lacking in goodwill toward all? I see how you could come to that conclusion. That's a fair cop. I can dig it. I don't particularly care in this particular instance, but I get it. I figure we've all been making resolutions for decades and, well, the world at a macro level is pretty shit, right? Climate change, housing, wealth disparity, income inequality, wage stagnation, benefits cliffs, Nazis in America, Nazis everywhere else, systemic racism, for-profit healthcare, and, well, the list goes on and on. Every year we get a little closer to death, a little less healthy, a little slower, a little foggier, mentally speaking, and it's just a slow decline toward shuffling off this mortal coil.

I should write Hallmark cards.

So! I figure I should try the New Year's wish thing. Resolutions just mean more work for me and wishing for things like world peace never really pan out, so just give me oodles of dough. Absurd amounts of bucks. It's not even like I want a second yacht to support my first yacht, a bunker fancier than Zuckerberg's, or a second private jet to follow around my first private jet. I'd just like to have more money than I know what to do with. The ability to pay for things and buy my way out of any trouble without having to think about it. Not have to put in a forty hour work week...and my job? My job is pretty darned cushy. I get paid a good amount of money to help people every day. It's a plum gig. But I'd still rather not have to spend 40+ hours of my week doing it.

Humans are capable of so much! We have so much creativity and imagination and intelligence and compassion and what do we have to do? Waste most of our lives working full-time. And that's if we're lucky! That's if we're not stitching together three jobs to try to make ends meet or literally slaving away as a child so people can have Nestlé chocolate bars. Instead of contributing to society through engineering projects and musical scores and...yeah, I understand that not everyone is going to be a Mozart and someone will have to do actually do important things like building and maintaining infrastructure and collecting trash, but gosh darnit all to heck we are so close to robots being able to do that kind of work anyway.

And yes, I understand you may think I hold the small, foolish beliefs of a child, but to that I just say, "You're a poopyhead."

Why "monetarily speaking?" Let's face it, I'm a straight, able-bodied, white, middle-aged, middle-class, heterosexual, Cis-male in America. I have an amazing wife and an amazing cat, a nice roof over my head, and can still afford to buy groceries. I'm already absurdly rich in many ways. I just need piles of filthy lucre to finish off that bingo card. Since it's the new year now, raise a glass of whatever you're drinking and make a wish for yourself. May you have wealth, prosperity, and abundance - and may I have wads of cash - in the new year.

#Family

Took Tenacious C to a cardiology appointment. Last minute cancellation yesterday afternoon for them meant we got the call to fill the spot and take her today. A month earlier than the earliest appointment we could get previously. Yay team.

Little One has a heart murmur. The white coats wanted to see if it was the kind where there were major heart issues or the kind that just meant she's getting older and everything's basically okay. Evidently when kitties have a heart murmur it's a fifty/fifty chance. Thank goodness it was the latter. Slight thickening of the wall in the left ventricle due to age, the doc said. Every time they said "due to age" I wanted to cover Sweet Pea's ears. She'd been drugged, twice, forced to fast since midnight, see a vet, and ride in a car for three hours. That's a very bad day for Gatta Bianca. I figured calling her old over and over again was a bit harsh. The white coats were pretty darned good, though. They almost always are at the Lois Bates Acheson Veterinary Teaching Hospital.

Now that we're home, Great White is fighting the effects of the sedation drugs she was given. She does this by jumping up onto everything she can then jumping back down again. Little daredevil. When she walks she sometimes missteps, but never when she's jumping. And her monkeys? We threw on Big Trouble in Little China, a movie we haven't sat down to watch in years. Very entertaining.

#Library

One of the perils of working in a public library is the number of patrons who try to recommend books to you. It's sweet. It's kind. It's thoughtful. It means that you are making a connection with the patron. Making them feel comfortable. This is a good thing. And I wish to the flying spaghetti monster in beer volcano heaven above that they'd stop.

First of all, being around books and patrons all day, my to-be-read list is already, like, six feet high. There are an ungodly number of books I have yet to get to.

Second, here's the first paragraph from a recommendation from a patron:

"Hands on her hips, youthful breasts outthrust beneath the bulk of her red-and-gray sweater, seventeen-year-old Roxanne Brianna O'Brien, captain of the Bisbee High School pep squad, tossed her long blonde hair and led her six-member team in a strutting parade around the end of the football field."

That's from JA Jance's, Skeleton Canyon, in case your interested in checking it out.

Patrons like all kinds of books and genres. This is wonderful and how things should be. It would be very uncool to make fun of someone for what they read. Jerk move. That being said, when I forget to do my due diligence and just grab a recommended book without vetting it for myself first, it can be awful to discover - once I'm in my comfy chair with my yummy snacks and drinks - that the book is something I have no interest in at all...so I just sit there, the tension in my body making my comfy chair slightly less comfortable, my fizzy yummy drinks slowly going flat and my munchies slowly turning stale, and the book case with all of my old standbys is way over there across the room and WHY IS LIFE SO UNFAIR!!!

#Humanity

The greatest barrier to ending racism? Not members of the KKK. Not members of the Proud Boys. Me. Just an average, everyday white person.

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. knew it.(1) Reni Eddo-Lodge knows it.(2)(3) Rachel Cargle knows it.(4) James Baldwin knew it.(5)(6) Ta-Nehisi Coates knows it.(7) Leonard Pitts knows it.(8) Lorraine Hansberry knew it.(9) Sam Reason knows it.(10) Dr. Maya Angelou knew it.(11) Cristina García knows it.(12) Dr. Roxane Gay knows it.(13)

(1)

"I have almost reached the regrettable conclusion that the Negro's great stumbling block in the stride toward freedom is not the White Citizens Councillor or the Ku Klux Klanner but the white moderate who is more devoted to order than to justice; who prefers a negative peace which is the absence of tension to a positive peace which is the presence of justice; who constantly says, 'I agree with you in the goal you seek, but I can't agree with your methods of direct action.'"
- Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., Letter from Birmingham Jail (pdf)

(2)

"I can no longer have this conversation, because we're often coming at it from completely different places. I can't have a conversation with them about the details of a problem if they don't even recognise that the problem exists. Worse still is the white person who might be willing to entertain the possibility of said racism, but who thinks we enter this conversation as equals. We don't."
- Reni Eddo-Lodge, Why I'm no longer talking to white people about race

(3)

"Amid every conversation about Nice White People feeling silenced by conversations about race, there is a sort of ironic and glaring lack of understanding or empathy for those of us who have been visibly marked out as different for our entire lives, and live the consequences. It's truly a lifetime of self-censorship that people of colour have to live. The options are: speak your truth and face the reprisals, or bite your tongue and get ahead in life. It must be a strange life, always having permission to speak and feeling indignant when you're finally asked to listen. It stems from white people's never-questioned entitlement, I suppose."
- Reni Eddo-Lodge, Why I'm no longer talking to white people about race

(4)

"White people love to be the victim in the conversation about race."
- Rachel Cargle, I Refuse to Listen to White Women Cry

(5)

"Whatever white people do not know about Negroes reveals, precisely and inexorably, what they do not know about themselves."
- James Baldwin, Letter from a Region in My Mind

(6)

"...and this is the crime of which I accuse my country and my countrymen, and for which neither I nor time nor history will ever forgive them, that they have destroyed and are destroying hundreds of thousands of lives and do not know it and do not want to know it. But it is not permissible that the authors of devastation should also be innocent. It is the innocence which constitutes the crime."

(9)

"We have to find some way with these dialogues to show and to encourage the white liberal to stop being a liberal and become an American radical."
