Italians Get Leaning Tower Of Pisa Wrong Again
Once again, Italians have proven they are lovers, not thinkers. For years, the ground under the Leaning Tower of Pisa had been giving way, so engineers had tied cables to it to keep it from falling while they reinforced the ground underneath. After years of renovations, the engineers removed the cables - but the tower still leans.

"We do not understand what happened," said Antonio Buscelli, one of the engineers who worked on the tower. "We reinforced the ground just fine, the tower is no longer in danger of falling over, but the damn thing just won't go completely vertical."

Actually...
It turns out that money actually does grow on trees. Bill Gates, the billionaire from Microsoft was out planting trees in his backyard with his ten-year-old daughter. When his daughter heard him mumbling, under his breath, "I wish money grew on trees", she asked him why it didn't. He didn't have a good answer, so he and his little girl planted a $100 bill and waited a few months. The $100 bill did indeed grow into a tree with $100 bills as leaves. "I'm just so happy I could cry," said Mr. Gates, "I mean, now I'll be even richer - how great a thing is that?"
