Ope...a Life



Basketball

7th grade basketball game. Player steps on my foot. Pain is so bad I sit in closest stands and cry. For two hours. In my uniform. Nobody comes to help until my parents pick me up. X-ray shows multiple metatarsal fractures.




NHL

NHL players are totes adorbs. They group hug for celebrations, chew their mouth guards like pacifiers, and every once in a while they throw a big tantrum and get put in a timeout.




Dixie Cup

I interviewed for a programmer position at a life insurance company. Because that's how desperate I was for a job. The final question? "What are five things you can do with a Dixie Cup?"

"Really?" I said.




UPS Guy

During the first month of COVID lockdown, I took to eating a bowl of cereal by the big picture window in the morning. I frequently saw the UPS guy. We'd wave and smile. One day he had a delivery for me. I opened the door. Big smiles. He tried handing me the package. My smile faltered. His smile faltered. He put the package on the stoop. I stopped eating cereal by the big picture window.




Together

I went to grade school with Perry. Perry told me, "Fart sounds are your butt cheeks flapping." Perry died of cancer.




Sorority

Calling my parents from college.

"I'll be coming home Friday night."

"Wonderful! Need to do some laundry, huh?"

"Ha! No. Gotta grab my suit. I was invited to a sorority formal."

"Really?"

"Yeah. It's some fancy to-do at a house on the Gold Coast."

"..."

"So I'll drive back Saturday morning. The formal is Saturday night."

"Are you sure they didn't invite you as a prank?"




K.I.T.T.

My parents needed a car. They took me along with them to a Pontiac dealership. I was nine. The car salesman asked me if I wanted to meet K.I.T.T..

Yes!

He led me to a car on the sales floor. There was no red flashy thing scrolling back and forth at the front. There were no cool dash lights reading "guidance system" and "ignitors." There was no "rocket boost" or "grappling hook" button. No Atari-looking steering wheel. The car didn't talk.

The salesman wouldn't stop smiling.
